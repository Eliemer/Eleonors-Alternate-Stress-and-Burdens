** Stress
In addition to *HP* and *Armor*, your pilot has a *Stress* bar,
represented by an 8-segment clock.

#+BEGIN_QUOTE
The final concept added to narrative play when using bonds is
STRESS. STRESS represents wear and tear experienced by pilots, both
physical and mental. It is tracked using an eight-segment clock.
-----
STRESS represents not only physical harm, but also
exhaustion, fatigue, mental or emotional suffering,
and so on. As such, PCs can take stress in a range of
situations, including ones that aren’t violent or phys-
ically dangerous (e.g., social situations, gathering
information, etc).
  ---Field Guide: The Karrakin Trade Baronies, p.88
#+END_QUOTE

** Resistance rolls
On receiving a Consequence, you may automatically ignore all of it, at
the cost of ~6 - Xd6kh1~ Stress, that is, roll ~Xd6~ where ~X~ is
number of relevant skills and take the /highest/ result. Regardless of
how many points of each skill you may have, only the number of
relevant skills counts. This does not mean you succeed a check, but
rather that you narrowly avoided the consequences of failing said
check.

** Burdens
When you would take *Stress* that would exceed your maximum, you
*Break* and obtain a *Burden*, as detailed in _Field Guide: The
Karrakin Trade Baronies_ (p.89). In addition, once you obtain a
*Burden*, you will suffer an immediate negative effect that lasts
until the end of the current *Scene*, as well as a persistent negative
effect which lasts for as long as you have /at least/ that many
*Burdens*
